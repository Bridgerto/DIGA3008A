Final Website Reflection: First Website, New languages, time management and all...

Author: Reneilwe Thobejane
Posted: 13th June, 2025 Johannesburg, South Africa

Creating my first personal website was a challenging but rewarding experience that taught me a lot about web development, time management, and letting go of perfectionism. Throughout this process, I did my best to meet the requirements of each language (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript) and I believe I managed to achieve the basic expectations in each area.

When it comes to folder structure, I made sure to separate my concerns clearly. My HTML, CSS, and JavaScript files are stored in separate folders, which I believe helped maintain clarity and scalability in my project. I followed naming conventions that made it easy for me to locate and update files when needed, and my structure loosely reflects the hierarchy of my website's content.

In terms of semantic markup, I used appropriate HTML tags to give structure and meaning to my content. I aimed to express my interface and content elements correctly so that both the browser and the user could understand the purpose of each section. While I still need to deepen my understanding of how semantic HTML can improve accessibility, I’m aware of its importance and will definitely continue to grow in this area.

A weakness I picked up toward the end of my project was in font sizing and visual hierarchy. During a consultation, it was brought to my attention that the contrast between my headings and body text was too different. My headings appeared very large compared to the small paragraph text, which created an uncomfortable reading experience. This was something I had overlooked until the later stages of development, and it’s a lesson I’ll carry forward.

My CSS styling was something I paid a lot of attention to, and I generally enjoyed the process of designing the look and feel of my website. That said, I struggled with maintaining consistency, especially with my initially proposed colour palette and layout spacing. At times, my design would look cohesive, and then suddenly it would feel jarring or disconnected. I’ve realized this was due to working without a solid plan or design system in place. In future projects, I want to set clearer guidelines for myself upfront to help maintain consistency throughout.

When it comes to JavaScript, I used it in a very minimal way. I focused mainly on basic DOM manipulation and event handling, but I now see that there’s a lot more I could’ve explored. I was unsure of how to expand the interactivity further, which reflects my current gap in knowledge. I definitely want to grow my skillset in JavaScript so I can make future websites more dynamic and engaging.

One of the biggest reflections I have is about time management. I didn’t pace myself well throughout the project, and often found myself jumping between sections, one moment I’d be working on blogs, the next on the essay page, then on the portfolio. This scattered approach eventually left me feeling overwhelmed, which stalled my motivation and progress. I’ve come to see how important it is to work within a structured system, dividing tasks into manageable chunks and sticking to a routine.

Another key lesson was learning to let go of attachment to my initial design. I started the semester with wireframes that I felt very committed to. But as deadlines approached and new requirements came in, I had to adapt and I often found myself resisting that change for too long. In hindsight, I wish I had let go of certain design ideas earlier to make space for more realistic solutions.

Lastly, while I enjoyed the technical learning, I didn’t manage to make my site as accessible or responsive as I had hoped. I didn’t implement media queries to make it mobile-friendly, and this was less about lack of knowledge and more about running out of time. I understand the importance of accessibility and responsive design, and it’s definitely something I’ll prioritise in future projects.

Overall, I found this project incredibly valuable. It introduced me to the fundamentals of coding and gave me insight into what it means to bring ideas to life through web development. While there were hurdles, I never felt like I couldn’t get through them, I just had to keep learning, experimenting, and staying patient with myself.
