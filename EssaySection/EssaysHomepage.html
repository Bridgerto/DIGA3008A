Essay 2: The Illusion of Neutrality: Mapping the Gulf of America on a Digital Battleground

This essay analyses Google Maps' renaming of the Gulf of Mexico to “Gulf of America,” exposing how digital platforms support geopolitical agendas. It argues that such changes reflect digital colonialism and neocolonial power, where dominant nations reshape narratives, marginalise others, and reinforce systemic inequalities through seemingly neutral digital tools.

Final Website Reflection: First Website, New languages, time management and all...

This website reflection explores my journey of building a personal portfolio site as a third-year Digital Arts student. I reflect on how I met the foundational requirements for folder structure, semantic HTML, CSS styling, and basic JavaScript interactivity. While I successfully implemented modular design and separation of concerns, I struggled with font sizing, design consistency, and time management. I also recognised the need to improve my site's accessibility and responsiveness. The experience taught me valuable lessons in project planning, adaptability, and letting go of rigid design ideas. Overall, the project was a rewarding introduction to web development and coding fundamentals.
