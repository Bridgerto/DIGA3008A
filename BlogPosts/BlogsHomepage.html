BLOGS SECTION

Week 1 Blog

From First Push to Flash: A Journey Through the Web's Past and My Digital Future.

This blog traces my personal and creative journey into web development, beginning with the hands-on experience of setting up Git for my first website. From there, I reflect on The World-Wide Web (1994) by Tim Berners-Lee et al., exploring how the early ideals of openness, decentralization, and connectivity continue to shape our digital lives. Finally, I dive into Jay Hoffman's article on the history of Flash, examining how it transformed the web into a space of rich visual expression. Then explore what its rise and fall reveals about the evolution of online creativity.

Week 2 Blog

Sketching the Web: Tracing Moulthrop’s Hypertext Theory to My Website’s Structure, Design, and Inspiration.

This blog captures the early stages of my website creation journey, starting with a reflection on Stuart Moulthrop’s You Say You Want a Revolution?, which explores hypertext and its impact on media, authorship, and user interaction. I then walk through my design process, from organizing my folder structure and developing a clear, scalable URL scheme to sketching my first wireframes for layout and navigation. The final section highlights websites that inspire my visual and interactive approach, helping me think more critically about design aesthetics, user experience, accessibility, and the broader possibilities of storytelling on the web.

Week 3 Blog

Interaction Design: User Alignment, Mapping-out site's content, Experience, and Interface Elements.

In this blog, I reflect on my interaction design process by defining my user alignment and mapping out my website's content, user experience, and interface elements. Drawing from the course slides, I explore how these design choices support accessibility, clarity, and user goals. I also connect this planning exercise to my site's structure and URL scheme, highlighting the importance of semantic HTML in organizing content meaningfully for both users and search engines. Finally, I offer a brief update on my development progress for the week, noting key improvements and next steps in the design and coding phases.

Week 4 Blog

Shaping the Experience: Design Decisions and Reflections

In this blog, I share my progress on the design section of my website in preparation for next week's submission. I begin engaging with the Interaction Design (IxD) process, noting initial thoughts and observations that will evolve over time. I reflect on how my original wireframes compare to my current design direction, evaluating what's holding up and what needs refinement. Additionally, I review my experience working on Website 1, acknowledging the challenges I've faced, what I'm proud of achieving so far, and what I aim to improve going forward in both design and development.

Week 7 Blog

NetFlorist, Takealot, Showmax: Weighing UX/UI for Analysis

In this blog, I explore potential websites for my upcoming UX and UI analysis essay. I focus on three South African platforms: NetFlorist, Takealot, and Showmax. For each, I assess the pros and cons in terms of user experience and interface design. I examine factors such as layout clarity, ease of navigation, visual consistency, and overall usability. This comparison helps me narrow down the most suitable site for a critical analysis, based on both design strengths and areas that could be improved.

Week 9 Blog

Bridging the Digital Divide: Justice, Equality, and Inclusion in the Digital Age

In Blog 9, I critically engage with the works of Tataki & Glynos, Sambuli, and Kositany-Buckner to explore the persistent and evolving nature of the digital divide. I examine how structural inequalities shape access to digital technologies, interrogate the meaning of digital equality, and highlight the need for inclusive digital design grounded in social justice principles.

Week 10 Blog

Offline and Left Behind: Why the Internet Should Be a Human Right

In Blog 10, I closely analyse Spyros Kasapis's article on why internet access should be a fundamental right, especially in times of crisis like the COVID-19 pandemic. I explore how his ethical arguments expose digital inequality, while also critiquing the article's gaps, such as its lack of solutions and attention to digital literacy—within the wider debate on digital justice.

Week 11 Blog

More Glitter, Less Gap: Reimagining the Internet Through African Feminist Eyes

In Blog 11, I explore Sheena Magenya's vision of a feminist internet rooted in African experiences, joy, and resistance. Through the lens of the MFIAfrica gathering, I analyze how digital inequalities intersect with gender, race, and colonial histories. Drawing connections to thinkers like Sambuli and Kositany-Buckner, I argue that digital decoloniality requires more than access, it demands inclusive storytelling, radical imagination, and structural change to make digital spaces truly liberating.

Week 12 Blog

Coding With Care: Rethinking Design Through a Decolonial Lens

In Blog 12, I reflect on how coding is not neutral but a powerful form of communication shaped by our beliefs and systems. Inspired by Hanli Geyser and Abeba Birhane, I examine my own coding practice, from improving accessibility and cultural sensitivity to integrating modular JavaScript for better UX. I explore how algorithmic colonization impacts Africa and why building inclusive, ethical tech means centering local voices and decolonizing our digital assumptions.

Week 13 Blog

The Internet as a Moral Space: Building with Care, Not Just Code

In Blog 13, I explore what an ethical internet means to me, inspired by Roger Silverstone's concept of “proper distance” and Shani OGrady's reflections. I argue that the internet is a moral space where our choices, whether coding, posting, or designing, carry ethical weight. True digital ethics means creating inclusive, caring, and decolonial spaces where all voices matter, digital inequalities are challenged, and connection is guided by fairness, respect, and responsibility.

Week 14 Blog

Coding with Conscience: Weaving Justice into Art and Tech

In Blog 14, I reflect on how justice and ethics shape my creative and coding practices. Drawing on Values at Play by Flanagan, Howe, and Nissenbaum, I explore the importance of value-sensitive design and the need to build inclusive, thoughtful systems. From questioning bias in data to amplifying underrepresented stories, I commit to creating with care, staying mindful of impact, and ensuring that my work reflects empathy, fairness, and responsibility.
