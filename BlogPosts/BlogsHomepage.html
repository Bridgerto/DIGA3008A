Week 1 Blog

From First Push to Flash: A Journey Through the Web’s Past and My Digital Future.

This blog traces my personal and creative journey into web development, beginning with the hands-on experience of setting up Git for my first website. From there, I reflect on The World-Wide Web (1994) by Tim Berners-Lee et al., exploring how the early ideals of openness, decentralization, and connectivity continue to shape our digital lives. Finally, I dive into Jay Hoffman's article on the history of Flash, examining how it transformed the web into a space of rich visual expression. Then explore what its rise and fall reveals about the evolution of online creativity.

Week 2 Blog

Sketching the Web: Tracing Moulthrop’s Hypertext Theory to My Website’s Structure, Design, and Inspiration.

This blog captures the early stages of my website creation journey, starting with a reflection on Stuart Moulthrop’s You Say You Want a Revolution?, which explores hypertext and its impact on media, authorship, and user interaction. I then walk through my design process, from organizing my folder structure and developing a clear, scalable URL scheme to sketching my first wireframes for layout and navigation. The final section highlights websites that inspire my visual and interactive approach, helping me think more critically about design aesthetics, user experience, accessibility, and the broader possibilities of storytelling on the web.
