Section 2: Define Your User Alignment

For me, defining my user alignment begins with understanding the goal of my site. I want a website that is chic, elegant and encompasses a sophisticated experience. I want it to look neat and put together so that my user will be served first-class treatment. I want my target audience to be anyone who is interested in a niche that marries animation with fashion elements. This is because I take animation and I plan to show the skills I've acquired through the medium of fashion. I want my target audience to visit my site with the intent of exploring an innovation that takes two art forms that have been blended into a singular form. My biggest priority is to make my site easily accessible and easy to navigate. For anyone to receive first-class treatment, everything has to be made available at their disposal. I want my target audience to understand the site structure, view key information and constantly understand where they are. I also want a website that is versatile and is able to be accessed from various devices. When users go on my site for a source of inspiration as this will be a platform with high-quality visuals that display my portfolio work. More than anything, I want it to be a fun experience of exploring and viewing my work that consists of an industry-specific portfolio.
